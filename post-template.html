The Middle of the Work

May 2025

There's a moment in most projects where the excitement of starting has worn off and the relief of finishing hasn't arrived yet. Nobody talks about this part much. It's not glamorous. It's just work.

But I've started to think this middle stretch is where almost everything important actually happens. The brief is done. The concept is approved. What remains is a thousand small decisions — each one easy to make carelessly, each one quietly shaping whether the final thing feels like it was made with attention or just completed.

Most of those decisions don't show up in any framework. Nobody presents them in a deck. They happen in the thirty seconds before you send a file, or in the moment you notice something feels slightly off but you can't immediately say why. That instinct — and what you do with it — is the craft.

I'm interested in that instinct. Where it comes from, how you develop it, and why some people have it and most don't. Not because I've fully figured it out, but because it seems like the actual thing worth understanding about work.
